HCDE 533 - Digital Fabrication

Hello! I'm Sara!

I started the HCDE program in Autumn 2019 and "graduated" this past June 2022. However, I still have one more course, so I have been saving the best for last!

I live in Kirkland with my spicy cat named Chilipepper. I commute via the bus, so I might rush out early (~9:15pm) some days.

I graduated from Georgia Tech in 2016 with a BS in computer science with a concentration in HCI and web, and another BS in Chinese language and culture. I speak Mandarin and travel to Richmond, BC too often to each Chinese food. I work at Microsoft as a newly minted PM for the Bing at Work team. I am both thrilled and terrified to have this job! Prior to that I was still at Microsoft but working as a front-end developer in Professional Services, which after 5 years I was ready to make the jump to something new.

Read more on my About page.

Why digital fabrication? I have a background in software, specifically web dev, but my hands-on experience is unfortunately limited. I've always loved creating things with my hands but I have never really made the jump into machine and computer assisted work.

My artistry background is mostly in textiles. I love fashion design and sewing. I am still an amatuer, but am mostly comfortable around sewing machines.

Beyond sewing, I took HCDE 539 Physical Computing & Prototyping with Adi Azulay in fall 2021, which introduced me to soldering and working with microcontrollers. I was really able to flourish due to my technical background--it was always the physics of electronics that was throwing me off! By jumping right in via a prototyping course, I found myself much more comfortable in exploring new things.

That course was part 1 of my adventure into tangible work, and I consider HCDE 533 (this course) to be part 2.

I have also dabbled in laser cutting, glass cutting, and minor woodworking.

What do I hope to learn? I'm looking forward to learning 3D design software and 3D printing! I also want to learn further fabrication techniques using the laser cutter and CNC mill. I also am just looking for the umph that will help me overcome my fears of trying new things in the world of fabrication and prototyping.

What can I teach my peers? Anyone need coding help? I've got you! I am also reasonably comfortable with Git on the command line. Beyond that, I can assist in sewing and overall debugging. I can probably teach much more, but I'm not sure yet what skills will be pertitent to this class.
